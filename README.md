# Chinook Database – SQL Joins & Business Insights

This SQL script demonstrates the use of **INNER JOIN** and **LEFT JOIN** on the **Chinook music database** to analyze customers, sales, tracks, genres, and artists.

## **Purpose**
- Learn how to combine multiple tables using joins.
- Calculate revenue per track, genre, and artist.
- Identify top-selling tracks and high-spending customers.
- Find customers who have never purchased.

## **Tables Used**
- **Customers** – customer details
- **Invoices** – purchase orders
- **InvoiceLines** – items purchased
- **Tracks** – song details
- **Albums** – album information
- **Artists** – artist information
- **Genres** – music genres

## **Queries Included**
1. List all customers who have made purchases (INNER JOIN)
2. Find customers who never placed orders (LEFT JOIN)
3. Total revenue per track
4. Total revenue per genre
5. Total revenue per artist
6. Total sales by country and date range
7. Top-selling tracks by quantity
8. Customers and their total spending
9. Tracks with genre and artist info

## **Instructions**
1. Load the Chinook database in your SQL tool (SQLite, MySQL, or PostgreSQL).
2. Run the queries to explore sales and customer data.
3. Export results to CSV if needed for reporting.
